1.Bored to death

Meaning/Usage:Very bored

Explanation:Death is the worst thing,so using it to compare how you feel is telling someone that you are very bored.

(i)I have nothing to do.I'm bored to death.

(ii)I hate it when I'm bored to death.

(iii)Would you rather be super busy or bored to death?

Other Common Sentences

"I'm so bored,I could die."
"I'm dying of boredom."

2.You've got to be kidding

Meaning/Usage:This is used when a true statement is ridiculous.

Explanation:"Kidding" is the same as joking.
When a person hears something that is true,but it is not expected,
the person will use this idiom to express that the statement is ridiculous.

A:"Hey Jared.Mom told me to tell you that you shouldn't stay out too late."
B:"You've got to be kidding me.I'm 30 years old."

Other Common Sentences

"I really hope you're wrong."
"Please tell me it ain't so."

3.Sick and Tired

Meaning/Usage:Used when you no longer enjoy something because you have done it too many times.

Explanation:It is common to say,"I'm sick of it" or "I'm tired of it."Both of these are negatibe terms.
If you have to do something again,it will either make you feel sick or tired because you do not enjou it.

(i)"I'm sick and tired of eating the same thing for lunch everyday."
(ii)"I'm getting sick and tired of this song.They play it way too often at this club."
(iii)"I'm sick and tired of listening to him nag all the time."

Other Common Sentences

"I'm getting sick of this phone.I think I'll buy a new one."
I'm tired of the same routine.I need to find something new and different.

4.Call it a day

Meaning/Usage:Used to express that the work day is over.

Explanation:"Call it" is often times used to make a decision.
When you add "day" to this,then a person is making a decision to say that the day is over.
dThis is mostly used to end a working day.

(i)"Let's call it a day.I'm too tired to continue working."
(ii)"We can't continue working without Mike,so let's call it a day."
(iii)"It's already nine o'clock.Let's call it a day."

Other Common Sentences

"Let's turn in for the night."
"I think we should pick it up from the morning."

pick it up=continue

5.Get on one's nerves

Meaning/Usage:Used when someone or something is bothering you.

Explanation:Your body uses your "nerves" to feel any sensation such as a tap on the shoulder,a poke,a hot fire that is too close,or anything else.
In this phrases,"nerves" is not used in a physical way, but to express that they are feeling bothered by what is happening.

(i)"You're beginning to get on my nerves."
(ii)"Will you please stop doing that?It's getting on my nerves."
(iii)"His whining is getting on my nerves."

Other Common Sentences

"You're beginning to annoy me."
"His complaints are starting to bother me."

6.Couch potato

Meaning/Usage:Used when someone watches too much television.

Explanation:This was created for someone watching too much television because in some western countries, a person usually sits on the "couch" and eats "potato" chips while watching television.

(i)"My husband is a couch potato.He sits in front of the TV all day long."
(ii)"You have a huge belly because you're a couch potato."
(iii)"I should be more active and less of a couch potato."

Other Common Sentences

"Good luck trying to get his attention.He's glued to the television.

7.Read one's mind

Meaning/Usage:When someone says something that you were also thinking about.

Explanation:This is a stronger way of saying,"I was thinking of that too."

(i)"You read my mind."
(ii)"I was going to suggest that.You must have read my mind."

A:"Do you want to play pool?"
B:"You read my mind.That's exactly what I was thinking too."

8.Feel blue

Meaning/Usage:Feel sad

Explanation:Long time ago, blue was related to the rain.Whenever the fictional god Zeus was sad,he would make it rain(crying).

(i)"What a gloomy day. It makes me feel blue.
(ii)"Whenever I feel blue, I like to listen to upbeat music."
(iii)"Matt's feeling a little blue right now.Let's go cheer him up."

Other Common Sentences

"I feel a little depressed right now."
"I don't know why, but I just feel sad."
"Even thought it's a nice day, I feel gloomy inside."

9.Fender bender

Meaning/Usage:A small car accident

Explanation:"Fender" is the car body part that is right above the tire. "Bender" is derived from bend.Since the fender is a small part compared to the hood and door, and bend is not as bad as break or crack, combining these two indicates that the accident is very small. Like a small dent or scratch.

(i)"I got into a small accident. It was just a fender bender."
(ii)"I got into a fender bender in the parking lot."
(iii)"I can't believe the damage is going to cost me 800 bucks. It was just a fender bender."

Other Common Sentences

"It was a small accident."
"I rear ended him, but luckily there was no damage."

10.Go dutch

Meaning/Usage:Each person paying for themselves.For example,going Dutch on a date is indicating both guy and girl will pay for themselves instead of one buying for the other.

Explanation:Why "Dutch" is used in this way is not completely confirmed.One reason is because of Dutch doors that contained two equal parts.Another reason was due to the rivalry between the English and Dutch in the 17th century.

(i)"You don't have to pay for me.Let's go dutch today."
(ii)"I can't believe John didn't pay for me.We ended up going dutch on the whole date."
(iii)"I actually prefer going dutch because I don't want to feel indebt to anyone."

Other Common Sentences

"I'll pay half of it."
"Let's pay together."
"I didn't pay for her.We paid our own share."

11.Chicken

Meaning/Usage:Being afraid or scared

Explanation:Chickens are very timid, afraid or scared of many things. It is common to use animals to express traits such as "sly as a fox" or "strong as a bear."

(i)"There's nothing to be scared of.Don't be a chicken."
(ii)"Everyone thinks I'm a chicken because I didn't go bungee jumping with them."
(iii)"Stop being a chicken and just go."

Other Common Sentences

"I didn't know you were afraid of insects.You're a scaredy cat."
"He's a coward.He wouldn't even go on the rollercoaster ride."
"Jack is scared of everything.He's such a sissy."
